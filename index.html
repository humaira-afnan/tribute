Personal Information

  • Full Name: William Shakespeare
  • Birth: April 23, 1564 (traditionally celebrated), Stratford-upon-Avon, England
  • Parents: John Shakespeare (father), Mary Arden (mother)
  • Marriage: Anne Hathaway (1582)
  • Children:
    • Susanna Shakespeare (born 1583)
    • Twins Hamnet and Judith Shakespeare (born 1585)
  • Death: April 23, 1616, Stratford-upon-Avon
  • Burial: Holy Trinity Church, Stratford-upon-Avon

Career and Work

  • Early Life and "Lost Years" (1564-1592):
    • Little is known about Shakespeare’s early life.
    • Speculated that he worked as a schoolmaster, apprentice, or was involved in theater during his "lost years" (1585-1592).
  • Actor and Playwright in London (1592-1613):
    • First mentioned as an actor and playwright in 1592.
    • Became a key member of the Lord Chamberlain's Men (later the King’s Men), acting and writing for the company.
    • Acted in many of his plays, although details of his specific roles are unclear.
  • Major Plays:
    • Tragedies: Hamlet, Macbeth, Othello, King Lear, Romeo and Juliet
    • Comedies: A Midsummer Night’s Dream, Much Ado About Nothing, Twelfth Night, As You Like It
    • Histories: Henry V, Richard III, Henry IV (Parts 1 and 2)
    • Romances: The Tempest, The Winter’s Tale, Pericles
  • Poetic Works:
    • Wrote narrative poems such as Venus and Adonis (1593) and The Rape of Lucrece (1594).
    • Authored 154 sonnets, many exploring themes of love, beauty, politics, and mortality.
  • Business Ventures:
    • Part-owner of the Globe Theatre in London.
    • Accumulated wealth through his ownership in the King’s Men and property investments.

Later Years and Retirement (1613-1616):

  • Retired to Stratford-upon-Avon around 1613 after writing The Tempest.
  • Continued to manage his estate and invest in local property.
  • Died in 1616 at the age of 52, shortly after revising his will.

Legacy

  • First Folio (1623):
    • After his death, fellow actors John Heminges and Henry Condell compiled his works into the First Folio, preserving many of his plays for posterity.
  • Enduring Influence:
    • Shakespeare's works remain foundational to English literature and drama, inspiring countless adaptations, interpretations, and scholarly studies worldwide.
